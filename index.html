Analyst insights
Deep discounting is structural, not promotional
Median discount is exactly 50% — this is not seasonal. 95% of products carry 10%+ discounts, strongly suggesting systematic MRP inflation across the catalog.
Customer sentiment is broadly positive
88% of products rate ≥ 3.8★. The rating curve peaks at 4.1–4.3, indicating a consistently satisfied customer base across categories.
Higher discounts weakly predict lower ratings
Correlation of –0.155 between discount % and rating. Aggressive pricing may signal lower quality. Office Products — lowest discount (12.4%) — has the highest avg rating (4.31★).
Electronics drives value; budget is the volume segment
Electronics avg MRP is ₹10,127 — 6× higher than Computers & Accessories. Yet 39% of all products are priced below ₹500 after discount — the budget segment rules by count.
12 high-risk listings need audit
Products combining 70%+ discounts with sub-3.5★ ratings are likely misleading buyers. Smartwatches and USB accessories are the most common offenders.
Amazon's own brands dominate review volume
AmazonBasics HDMI cables top the chart with 4,26,973 reviews at just ₹219–₹309. Private label at budget prices is the engagement winner on the platform.
Review count distribution is heavily skewed
Mean reviews = 18,296 but median = 5,179 — a small number of viral products pull the average up. The top 10 products alone account for over 30 lakh reviews.
